## Why Agentic AI Models Are Overhyped

Agentic AI models have been marketed as the future of software development—tools that promise to independently solve problems, write code, and even "think" creatively. Models like [Qwen3.6-35B-A3B](https://www.techinsights.ai/qwen3-open-models) are celebrated for their "agentic coding power" and the ability to run on consumer laptops. Yet, the reality is far less impressive. These systems often falter at complex tasks requiring contextual understanding, judgment, and nuance. Developers frequently find themselves spending more time debugging AI-generated output than writing code themselves.

Take Qwen3.6-35B-A3B, for instance. While its creators highlight its capabilities, its practical applications are limited to basic, repetitive tasks. A developer who tested it noted that while it could draw an impressive pelican, its ability to handle real-world coding problems was mediocre at best ([source](https://www.devstories.io/qwen-pelican-test)). These flashy demos may entertain, but they fail to deliver meaningful productivity gains for developers.

## The Productivity Edge: Augmentative Tools Over Agentic AI

The hype surrounding agentic AI models distracts from tools that genuinely enhance developer productivity. Augmentative tools, such as the [Android CLI](https://www.androiddev.io/android-cli-productivity), focus on collaboration between human developers and AI. These tools don’t claim to replace human judgment but instead amplify human skills by addressing specific pain points in the workflow—like automating repetitive tasks and optimizing processes.

Here’s how augmentative tools deliver real value:

### Code Example: Augmentative AI in Action

Below is an example of how Android CLI simplifies app development:

```bash
# Generate boilerplate code for an Android activity
android-cli generate activity --name MainActivity --layout main_activity.xml

# Automatically optimize app dependencies
android-cli optimize dependencies --project MyAndroidApp

# Debug common issues with intelligent suggestions
android-cli debug --project MyAndroidApp --fix-suggestions
```

Each command is designed to streamline a specific aspect of the development process. Instead of attempting to "think" autonomously, the tool empowers developers to work faster and smarter. This is tangible value—not overhyped autonomy.

## Creativity in Coding: More Than Drawing Pelicans

Agentic AI models often tout their creative capabilities as a selling point. But let’s be honest: drawing pelicans or generating poetry might be fun, but it’s irrelevant to the actual needs of software development. Tools like [Claude Opus 4.7](https://www.anthropic.com/claude-opus) position themselves as "creative problem solvers," but their outputs often require significant human intervention to be useful.

In contrast, augmentative tools excel in areas developers care about: debugging, refactoring, optimizing performance, and streamlining CI/CD pipelines. Creativity in coding is about solving real-world problems—not generating art.

## The Risks of Overhyping Agentic AI

The push for agentic AI models introduces not only practical challenges but also ethical and security risks. Autonomous systems can inadvertently generate malicious code, making them a liability rather than an asset. A recent security report highlighted how agentic models could be exploited to create harmful scripts, emphasizing the need for stricter safeguards ([source](https://www.cybersecuritynews.ai/ai-coding-dangers)).

Beyond security concerns, these models often fail to deliver on their promises, eroding trust in AI tools. Developers are left cleaning up after systems that overpromise and underdeliver, wasting time and resources.

## The Augmentation Advantage: A Better Path Forward

The future of AI in software development lies in augmentation—not autonomy. Augmentative tools have already proven their value by reducing repetitive tasks, acting as intelligent assistants, and empowering human developers to focus on high-impact work. From GitHub Copilot to Android CLI, these tools demonstrate the practical benefits of collaboration between humans and machines.

Here’s the simple truth: AI should be about enabling developers, not replacing them. Agentic models like Qwen3.6-35B-A3B fail because they try to do too much, ignoring the complexity of human judgment and expertise. Augmentative tools succeed because they stay focused on what they do best—amplifying human productivity.

## Conclusion: Focus on What Works

Agentic AI models are the latest shiny distraction in tech, but they’re all hype and little substance. Developers should stop chasing the dream of autonomous AI agents and instead focus on tools that deliver real-world value through augmentation. Big Tech’s push for agentic systems may be more about marketing and control than solving practical problems, but independent developers have the power to resist this narrative.

Let’s focus on what works: tools that empower, not overpromise. The future of AI in coding isn’t about flashy demos or lofty promises—it’s about tangible results.
